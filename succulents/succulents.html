Echeveria

Echeveria varieties are loved for their rosette shapes and ease of care, making them perfect for indoor gardening.

  • Water: Water thoroughly when soil is dry to the touch, then allow to dry out completely.
  • Sunlight: Needs plenty of sunlight, preferably a few hours of direct sunlight each day.
  • Soil: A well-draining succulent mix is essential.
  • Fertilizing: Feed with a diluted low-nitrogen fertilizer during the growing season.

Image source: BigCommerce

Opuntia

Opuntias are easily recognized by their paddle-shaped arms. They are able to withstand both extreme cold and heat, and bear edible fruit.

  • Water: Water sparingly; overwatering can lead to root rot.
  • Sunlight: Thrives in full sun, with at least 6 hours of direct sunlight per day.
  • Soil: Use a gritty, well-draining soil mix.
  • Cold Tolerance: Some varieties can withstand frost; check the specific species for cold tolerance levels.

Image source: BigCommerce

Jade Plant

The Jade Plant is a symbol of good luck and prosperity. It prefers bright light and needs to dry out between waterings.

  • Water: Allow soil to dry completely before watering again.
  • Sunlight: Prefers full sun to partial shade.
  • Soil: Use a well-draining succulent mix.
  • Temperature: Keep at room temperature, and protect from drafts and extreme cold.

Snake Plant

The Snake Plant is known for its air-purifying abilities and is incredibly hardy, tolerating low light and infrequent watering.

  • Water: Water sparingly; soil should be allowed to dry between waterings.
  • Sunlight: Can tolerate low light but grows best in moderate to bright, indirect light.
  • Soil: Use a loose, well-draining potting mix.
