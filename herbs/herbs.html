Herbs

An informative guide to different types of herbs and their culinary and medicinal uses. For more info on how to take care of succulents, visit Everything You Need to Grow Your Own Herbs.

Image source: American Meadows

Thyme

Thyme is a versatile herb used in many cuisines. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil and needs little watering once established.

  • Water: Allow soil to dry between waterings.
  • Sunlight: Full sun.
  • Soil: Well-drained, preferably with a pH of 6.0 to 8.0.
  • Harvest: Pick leaves as needed. Best flavor just before flowering.

Image source: High Country Gardens

Oregano

Oregano is a staple in Italian and Greek cooking. It thrives in part to full sun and is drought-tolerant once established.

  • Water: Water when the top inch of soil feels dry.
  • Sunlight: Full sun to partial shade.
  • Soil: Well-drained with a pH of 6.0 to 8.0.
  • Pruning: Trim to encourage bushy growth. Harvest before the plant flowers for best flavor.

Image source: Giant Eagle

Parsley

Parsley is rich in vitamins and is used as a garnish in many dishes. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

  • Water: Keep the soil evenly moist.
  • Sunlight: Full sun to partial shade.
  • Soil: Rich, moist, well-drained soil.
  • Harvest: Cut leaves from the outer portions of the plant as needed.

Image source: Peoples Rx

Basil

Basil is a common herb used in Italian cuisine, famous for its role in dishes like pesto and Caprese salad. It has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

  • Water: Water regularly, keeping the soil slightly moist.
  • Sunlight: Prefers full sun.
  • Soil: Well-drained soil with a neutral pH.
  • Pruning: Pinch off flower buds to encourage leaf growth. Harvest leaves regularly.

Image source: American Meadows

Mint

Mint is known for its cool and refreshing taste. It's often used in beverages, jellies, and garnishes. Mint is also used for its digestive properties.

  • Water: Keep soil consistently moist.
  • Sunlight: Prefers full sun to partial shade.
  • Soil: Any well-drained, fertile soil.
  • Tip: Mint is invasive; consider planting in containers to control growth.

Image source: Little League Organic Farmers

Cilantro

Cilantro, also known as coriander leaves, is commonly used in Mexican and Asian cuisines. It has a very distinctive flavor and is often used fresh in salsas and curries.

  • Water: Water regularly to keep the soil moist but not soggy.
  • Sunlight: Grows best in full sun but can tolerate light shade.
  • Soil: Well-draining soil with a pH of 6.2 to 6.8.
  • Tip: Cilantro tends to bolt in hot weather; plant in spring or fall for best results.

Image source: High Country Gardens

Rosemary

Rosemary has a woody, citrus-like fragrance and is used for flavoring foods. It's also associated with improving memory and has anti-inflammatory properties.

  • Water: Allow soil to dry out between waterings; overwatering can lead to root rot.
  • Sunlight: Thrives in full sun.
  • Soil: Prefers well-draining, sandy soil with a pH of 6.0 to 7.5.
  • Pruning: Prune regularly to encourage growth and prevent woodiness.
