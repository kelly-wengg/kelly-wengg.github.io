Roses

Roses are widely appreciated for their beauty and scent. They symbolize love and are popular in bouquets, especially on romantic occasions.

  • Water: 1 inch per week.
  • Sunlight: At least 6 hours of direct sunlight per day.
  • Soil: Well-drained, fertile soil with a pH between 6.0 and 6.5.
  • Fertilizer: Balanced, slow-release fertilizer applied in early spring and midsummer.

Lilies

Lilies are revered for their large, showy blooms and are often associated with purity and renewal.

  • Water: Keep soil moist, especially during growth and bloom periods.
  • Sunlight: Full sun to partial shade.
  • Soil: Well-draining soil rich in organic matter.
  • Fertilizer: Use a high-potassium fertilizer every few weeks during the growing season.

Image source: American Meadows

Tulips

Tulips are identifiable by their bold colors and distinct shape, making them a springtime favorite.

  • Water: Moderate watering, allowing the soil to dry out between waterings.
  • Sunlight: Full sun is ideal for the best blooms.
  • Soil: Well-drained, sandy soil.

Daisies

Daisies can be identified by their iconic white petals surrounding a yellow disc. They are low maintenance.

  • Water: 1 inch per week, more during hot, dry periods.
  • Sunlight: Full sun to partial shade.
  • Soil: Average, well-draining soil.
  • Fertilizer: Light feeding with a general-purpose fertilizer once a month during the growing season.

Image source: American Meadows

Orchids

Orchids are exotic plants that come in many varieties, recognizable by their symmetrical flowers.

  • Water: Once a week during winter, twice a week during summer.
  • Sunlight: Bright, indirect sunlight.
  • Humidity: High humidity, between 40% and 70%.
  • Soil: Special orchid potting mix, allowing for good drainage and aeration.
